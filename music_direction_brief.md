# Music Direction Brief — The Missing Ancestor
## Thinking like a film score composer, not a playlist maker

---

## THE CORE PROBLEM WITH BLANKET MUSIC

A single track at constant volume does three bad things:
1. **Competes with voice** during dense information passages (1:04–1:51, 4:04–5:35)
2. **Wastes emotional ammunition** — if music is always at 15%, it can't go UP when it matters
3. **No hook, no drop** — the biggest lines ("Every person is African", "They found the skull") land the same as filler lines

The fix isn't a different piece of music. It's **an automation curve** — music that breathes with the script.

---

## THE THREE-MOVEMENT ARCHITECTURE

Rather than 7 separate files, think of ONE continuous score in 3 movements:

### Movement 1 — "The Inquiry" (0:00 → 2:50)
- **Texture**: Sparse. Single instruments. Cool tones. Clock motif.
- **Instruments**: Low drone (cello/synth pad), sparse piano stabs, ticking pulse, faint kalimba
- **Emotional arc**: Mystery → curiosity → tension building toward revelation
- **Music's job here**: Set the detective thriller tone WITHOUT competing with information

### Movement 2 — "The Evidence" (2:51 → 5:35)
- **Texture**: African instruments emerge. The score warms up as physical evidence appears.
- **Instruments**: Djembe (enters at the skull discovery), duduk/bamboo flute melody, kora (for Lucy section)
- **Emotional arc**: Shock of discovery → accumulation of proof → quiet wonder
- **Music's job here**: React to the Toumaï revelation. Then step back HARD for the dense evidence sections.

### Movement 3 — "The Verdict" (5:36 → 8:06)
- **Texture**: Full palette. Thriller twist first, then resolution.
- **Instruments**: All elements return and combine. Ends with full harmonic resolution.
- **Emotional arc**: Red herring tension → Darwin vindication → verdict → profound close
- **Music's job here**: Carry the emotional landing. The biggest musical moment is the LAST 30 seconds, not the skull reveal.

## THE HOOK AND DROP MAP

### What is a HOOK?
A moment where music rises ABOVE the voice, briefly, to set tone or punctuate a shift.
Only use before voices enter or in instrumental-only moments.

### What is a DROP?
A deliberate silence (music goes to 0%) that makes the NEXT line hit harder.
These are your most powerful tool. Use sparingly — max 3-4 per video.

## THE 8 HIT POINTS (specific moments for musical accents)

A hit point is when the music physically reacts to what's on screen — a strike, a swell, a sudden quiet.

1. **0:20** — Musical breath/drop at "Every person is African"
2. **1:16** — Small accent: "The DNA clock" (ticking motif enters)
3. **2:35** — Tension peak: "seven million year old ghost"

## THE DEEP DUCK ZONES (where your blanket track was wrong)

These are the sections where music must be NEARLY INAUDIBLE (6-8%):

| Zone | Time | Why |
|------|------|-----|
| DNA clock explanation | 1:04–1:50 | Two hosts trading technical information — any music competes |
| Calibration section | 2:01–2:17 | Complex scientific method explanation |
| Lucy → Habilis detail | 4:04–5:21 | Longest information run — 77 seconds of dense content |
| Darwin + Java Man | 5:49–6:39 | Historical narrative, lots of names and dates |

**Rule: any time both hosts are exchanging back-and-forth rapidly → 8% maximum.**

## WHAT TO ACTUALLY GENERATE

### Option A: One continuous 8-minute score
- Tell Lyria (or Suno/Udio): generate an 8-minute ambient documentary score
- Then apply the automation curve in CapCut / DaVinci Resolve as volume keyframes
- Most accurate to this brief

### Option B: Three movement files with crossfades
- Inquiry (0:00–2:50), Evidence (2:51–5:35), Verdict (5:36–8:06)
- Crossfade 2-3 seconds at each boundary
- Apply automation curve within each file

### Option C: Atmosphere base + hit point stings (RECOMMENDED)
- **1 long ambient atmosphere track** (full 8 min, very low 8-10%) — the invisible floor
- **8 short sting files** (0.5–3s each) for the hit points — fired on exact frames
- **1 resolution track** (1:30 long) for the final close (7:33–end)
- In CapCut: atmosphere on Track 2, stings on Track 3, resolution on Track 4
- This gives you the most control and is the most professional result

## THE SINGLE MOST IMPORTANT THING

The video's emotional peak isn't the Toumaï skull reveal (3:13). 
It's the last 20 seconds (7:44–8:06) — "one ancient, undivided human family."

For a Malayalam-speaking, religious audience, this line is the entire point of the video. 
**The music must save everything it has for those 20 seconds.**

Everything before 7:44 is loading the gun. The final line fires it.
