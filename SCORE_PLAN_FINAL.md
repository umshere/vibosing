## THE LEITMOTIF

One simple idea that binds the whole score:

**A 3-note kalimba phrase.** Rising. Like a question.

- **First appearance** (0:22): After "every person is African." Bare. Just the 3 notes and silence. Plants the seed.
- **Second appearance** (1:52): During the ghost-hunting section. Same 3 notes but now over the heartbeat pulse. Growing.
- **Third appearance** (3:18): After the skull is found. Same 3 notes but now played on duduk/flute over djembe. Transformed. Ancient.
- **Fourth appearance** (7:44): The final resolution. Same 3 notes but now fully harmonised, cello + kora + strings. RESOLVED. The question is answered.

This motif tells a subconscious story: the same musical question asked in four different ways, answered only at the end.

## THE 12 SECTIONS FOR GENERATION

These prompts are written for the subtext, not the text. Density is controlled. Leitmotif is embedded. Frequency awareness is built in.

### Section 1 — The Threshold (0:00–0:20)
**Density:** Full → Bed
**Subtext:** Something is about to change what you believe
**Prompt:**
```
Dark cinematic opener. Sub-bass drone (40-80Hz) with a single high 
kalimba playing 3 ascending notes — the motif. Sparse piano stab. 
60 BPM. Ducks immediately at 5 seconds to near-silence. The kalimba 
motif hangs in reverb. No melody. No rhythm section. No mid-range 
instruments. Ends in a whisper of drone.
```

### Section 2 — The Setup (0:20–0:49)
**Density:** Bed (minimal)
**Subtext:** Your skepticism has an expiration date
**Prompt:**
```
Almost nothing. A barely-audible low string pad (cello, below 200Hz). 
Occasional high-register texture (above 4kHz) — glass-like chimes. 
No rhythm. No melody. Atmospheric only. Should feel like the air 
before a storm. 55 BPM feel. Must not compete with dense dialogue.
```

### Section 3 — The Forensics (0:50–1:51)
**Density:** Bed (minimal)
**Subtext:** Science working in the dark
**Prompt:**
```
Ticking clock texture — a single woodblock or rimshot pulse at 72 BPM. 
Sub-bass drone continues underneath. Faint electronic shimmer above 
5kHz. Feels like a lab at 3am. Cold, precise, searching. The ticking 
is almost subliminal. No harmonic movement. No melody. Purely textural.
```

### Section 4 — The Hunt (1:52–2:50)
**Density:** Bed → Transitional
**Subtext:** Something is out there. We're closing in.
**Prompt:**
```
The ticking evolves into a heartbeat — low bass pulse at 65 BPM. 
Tension building. Sustained low strings begin harmonic movement 
(unresolved suspensions). A single kalimba note from the motif 
appears once at the midpoint. Growing sense of inevitability. Still 
no melody. No mid-range. The pulse is the music. Ends unresolved.
```

### Section 5 — The Convergence (2:51–3:12)
**Density:** Transitional (rising)
**Subtext:** 7 million years meeting the present
**Prompt:**
```
Building. The heartbeat pulse accelerates slightly (68 BPM). Low 
strings add a second voice. Faint African percussion enters — a 
single distant djembe. The kalimba motif's second note appears high 
in the mix. Everything pointing toward something. Harmonically 
suspended — never resolves. Cuts to complete silence at the end.
```

### Section 6 — The Skull (3:13–3:18)
**Density:** Silence → Sting
**Subtext:** Vindication
**Prompt:**
```
2 seconds of absolute silence. Then one single deep frame drum hit 
with 3-second reverb tail. Nothing else. The hit should feel like 
the earth remembering. Low frequency only (below 150Hz). No melody. 
No harmony. Just the hit and its echo.
```

### Section 7 — Sacred Ground (3:18–4:03)
**Density:** Transitional
**Subtext:** Holding something ancient and irreplaceable
**Prompt:**
```
The kalimba motif returns — all 3 notes — but now played on a duduk 
or bamboo flute. Slow, rubato (no strict tempo). A djembe provides 
gentle pulse underneath. Low drone continues. The melody is ancient, 
not triumphant. Feels like unearthing something that has been waiting 
to be found. 55 BPM feel. Warm but restrained.
```

### Section 8 — The Evidence Unfolds (4:04–5:35)
**Density:** Bed (minimal)
**Subtext:** Piece after piece, the story writes itself
**Prompt:**
```
Kora (West African harp) playing a gentle, repeating 2-bar pattern. 
Very sparse — one plucked note every 2 seconds. Light sustained 
string pad below. No rhythm section. No percussion. Absolute minimum 
density. Must disappear completely under dialogue. Think: sunlight 
through a window. Present but not performing. 65 BPM.
```

### Section 9 — The Twist (5:36–6:53)
**Density:** Bed (shifted texture)
**Subtext:** Misdirection corrected. Truth is stubborn.
**Prompt:**
```
Texture shift — the warm kora is replaced by cooler tones. Pizzicato 
strings (high register, above 4kHz). Slight dissonance in the pad. 
Not dramatic — just unsettled. Feels like looking at the map and 
realising you've been reading it wrong. 70 BPM. Minimal. The strings 
gradually warm back up toward the end of this section.
```

### Section 10 — The Verdict Builds (6:53–7:22)
**Density:** Transitional (building)
**Subtext:** Certainty arriving — not a fanfare, a deep breath
**Prompt:**
```
Everything slowly gathering. The kora returns. The heartbeat pulse 
returns. Low strings rising. Each element re-entering one by one, 
building a wave that doesn't break. The kalimba motif's first 2 
notes appear again — the question, still unanswered. Momentum 
without climax. 72 BPM, steady. Ends with a 2-second fade to silence.
```

### Section 11 — The Resolution (7:33–7:57)
**Density:** FULL
**Subtext:** We are one. All division is invented.
**Prompt:**
```
THE PEAK. Everything arrives. The kalimba motif plays in FULL — 
all 3 notes, harmonised with cello and kora together. African 
percussion (djembe, frame drum) provides a grounded heartbeat. 
Strings swell underneath. This is the only moment in 8 minutes 
where the full palette plays. Warm, profound, unhurried. Not 
triumphant — tender. 55 BPM. Like an embrace. Not a victory march.
```

### Section 12 — Stillness (7:57–8:06)
**Density:** Full → Fade
**Subtext:** Let it settle. Remember this.
**Prompt:**
```
The instruments fade one by one. Percussion stops first. Strings 
thin to a single cello note. The kalimba plays the motif one final 
time — alone, as it was at the beginning, but now resolved. Slower. 
Lower. Answered. Fades to silence over 6 seconds. The last sound 
should be the reverb tail of the final kalimba note disappearing.
```

## FREQUENCY GUIDELINES

The two hosts' voices occupy 200Hz–4kHz. The score lives OUTSIDE this range:

| Instrument | Frequency zone | When used |
|------------|---------------|-----------|
| Sub-bass drone | 40–120Hz | Throughout (always under voice) |
| Cello (low register) | 80–250Hz | Sections 4, 7, 10, 11 |
| Djembe | 80–200Hz + 6kHz attack | Sections 5, 6, 7, 11 |
| Kalimba | 1–2kHz (brief) + 4–8kHz harmonics | Leitmotif moments only |
| Duduk/flute | 400Hz–1.5kHz (conflicts! use sparingly) | Section 7 only, during low-speech |
| Kora | 200Hz–1.5kHz (conflicts! keep very low volume) | Section 8, 10, 11 only |
| Strings (high) | 4kHz+ harmonics | Sections 9, 11 |
| Chimes/glass | 5kHz+ | Sections 2, 3 |

**Rule:** Mid-range instruments (duduk, kora) only appear when speech density is lowest.
